About Me

My name is Alex and I'm a junior computer science major at UC Berkeley. What fascinates me most about CS is the vast diversity of new problems just waiting to be taken on. My interest are in machine learning, data science and backend devlopment. Experience

  •

    CellScope

    Research Intern

    As a developer at CellScope, I took on roles as both a software and machine learning engineer. I wrote a script that took uploaded image data, stitched them together to make videos, and created training data from cropped regions of interest in the video for analysis in a convolutional neural network. I developed a web application which supplemented this script for viewing these images and videos of blood samples from Amazon S3. On the other side of my role, I trained and optimized a convolutional neural network to count malaria eggs in cell images. The algorithm is deployed on an AWS EC2 instance for live classification.

  • Berkeley Institute for Data Science

    Modules Developer

    At BIDS, we are developing iPython Notebooks focused on teaching data science for various courses. The first module I developed was for L&S 88-5 (Rediscovering Text as Data). The notebook introduced how to use the HaitiTrust API for NLP tasks. Using metadata from several volumes, I walked through how to train models using Scikit Learn to classify book genre and author gender. I will be presenting my tutorial at the HTRC UnCamp 2018 at UC Berkeley.

  • Commvault

    Software Engineering Intern

    During my time at Commvault, I developed the Commvault Video Search engine, a service that plays and searches videos by their transcribed audio content. I accomplished this by using the Microsoft Azure Media Indexer to generate the video transcriptions, and then fed it to a database in the Commvault Analytics Engine for querying. I also wrote a web application that complemented this service for uploading and streaming these videos. In this app, a user can search for a particular query and the video will play from the point where the search term is mentioned.

  • UC Berkeley

    Lab Assistant for CS61A

    I tutored students during labs and office hours for CS61A (Structure & Interpretation of Computer Programs), the introduction computer science class at Berkeley. Topics include: inheritance, recursion, trees, linked lists, and streams. Taught in Python, SQL, and Scheme.
